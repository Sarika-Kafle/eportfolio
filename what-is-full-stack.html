What is Full Stack Development?

Full stack development is the practice of building both the client-side (frontend) and server-side (backend) of a web application. Think of it like being able to work on both sides of a restaurant - you can cook in the kitchen (backend) and also serve customers at the front (frontend). A full stack developer understands how the browser renders HTML/CSS/JS, how APIs expose business logic, and how databases persist and retrieve data.

Frontend

  • HTML for semantic structure and accessibility - it's like the skeleton of your website that gives it proper meaning and helps screen readers understand the content.
  • CSS for layout, typography, and responsive design - this is what makes your website look beautiful and adapt to different screen sizes, from phones to desktop computers.
  • JavaScript for interactivity and client-side logic - this adds the "magic" that makes buttons work, forms validate, and pages feel dynamic and engaging.

Backend

  • Runtime & Frameworks (e.g., Node.js & Express) to serve requests and implement business logic - this is like the engine that powers your website behind the scenes, handling user requests and processing data.
  • APIs (REST/JSON) to communicate between client and server - think of this as the language that the frontend and backend use to talk to each other, like a waiter taking orders between the kitchen and dining room.
  • Databases (SQL or NoSQL) to store and query data efficiently - this is where all your website's information lives, whether it's user accounts, blog posts, or product catalogs.
