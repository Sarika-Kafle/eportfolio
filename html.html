Semantic HTML & Accessible Forms

This page demonstrates semantic structure using header, nav, main, section, and footer, along with an accessible contact form. Semantic HTML is like using the right words when speaking - instead of saying "that thing over there," you say "the red car." It helps both people and computers understand what your content means.

Why Semantic HTML Matters

When you use semantic HTML, you're telling the browser exactly what each part of your page is for. A <header> isn't just a div with some styling - it's the introduction to your page. A <nav> is clearly a navigation menu. This makes your website more accessible to screen readers and helps search engines understand your content better.

<header>
  <h1>My Portfolio</h1>
  <nav>...</nav>
</header>
<main>
  <section>...</section>
</main>
<footer>...</footer>
